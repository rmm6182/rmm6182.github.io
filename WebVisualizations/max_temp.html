Maximum Temperature Visual

This visualization shows the comparison of Latitude to Maximum Temperature. Latitude is on the X-axis (bottom axis). Temperature, which is in Fahrenheit, is on the Y-axis (left axis). There is an interesting relationship between Latitude and the Maximum Temperature associated with that region. The trend that is shown is an Inverse Quadratic Function (y = -x^2). Also known as a Parabola.

This relationship shows increasing higher temperatures the closer you get to the Equator (Latitute = 0). As the Latitude goes further away from the Equator on either side, the Temperature decreases.
